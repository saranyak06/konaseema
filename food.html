Ariselu (Athirasalu)

Ariselu is a rice-based sweet made using rice flour, jaggery, and ghee or oil. It has been prepared in Andhra Pradesh for hundreds of years, especially in rural areas where rice and jaggery were easily available. The recipe is usually passed down from grandmothers to mothers to daughters, making it a family tradition.

Gavvalu

Gavvalu is famous in Andhra Pradesh because of its distinct shell shape, simple preparation, festive importance, and crunchy sweetness. It represents togetherness, tradition, and the joy of homemade festival sweets.

Sunnundalu

Sunundalu is a famous traditional sweet from Andhra Pradesh. It is a healthy and energy-rich laddu made mainly from urad dal (black gram) and jaggery or sugar. Sunundalu is well known for its nutritional value as well as traditional importance.

Bobbatlu / Bakshalu

Bobbatlu is famous in Andhra Pradesh because it represents festivals, family traditions, and rich Telugu cuisine. More than just a sweet, it is an emotion connected with celebrations and togetherness.

Boondi Laddu & Murukulu

Boondi Laddu is a traditional Indian sweet made from deep-fried gram flour pearls (boondi) soaked in sugar syrup and shaped into spheres. In the Konaseema region of Andhra Pradesh, it is particularly famous due to the area's rich tradition of artisanal sweet-making and the specific use of high-quality ingredients like pure ghee and edible camphor, which give it a distinct "temple-style" aroma similar to the world-famous Tirupati Laddu.
