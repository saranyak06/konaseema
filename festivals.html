Festivals of Konaseema

Sankranti

Sankranti is the most important harvest festival of Konaseema. People decorate their homes with rangoli, enjoy traditional foods, and visit Ainavilli, Antharvedi, Bhimavaram Kodipandhalu, Jaganathota Prabhala Theertham, and nearby beach festivals.

Ugadi

Ugadi marks the Telugu New Year. The special Ugadi Pachadi is prepared using six ingredients that represent different emotions of life such as happiness, sorrow, anger, fear, surprise, and hope.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated for nine days with devotion. Beautiful idols of Lord Ganesha are installed, prayers are offered, and cultural programs are organized across Konaseema.

Devi Navaratri

Devi Navaratri is celebrated for twelve nights in Konaseema. Devotees worship Goddess Durga in various forms and participate in spiritual, religious, and cultural activities.

Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, symbolizes the victory of good over evil. Homes are decorated with lamps, sweets are shared, and fireworks light up the celebrations across the region.
