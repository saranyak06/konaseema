Festivals of Konaseema

Sankranti

Sankranti is the most important harvest festival of Konaseema. People decorate their homes with rangoli, enjoy traditional foods, and visit famous places like Ainavilli, Antharvedi, Bhimavaram Kodipandhalu, Jaganathota Prabhala Theertham, and nearby beach festivals.

Ugadi

Ugadi marks the Telugu New Year. The special Ugadi Pachadi is prepared with six ingredients representing nine types of life tastes such as happiness, sadness, anger, fear, surprise, and hope.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated for nine days with devotion. Beautiful idols of Lord Ganesha are installed, prayers are performed, and cultural programs are organized across Konaseema.

Devi Navaratri

Devi Navaratri is celebrated for twelve nights in Konaseema. Devotees worship Goddess Durga in different forms and participate in spiritual and cultural activities.

Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, celebrates the victory of good over evil. It is associated with the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya. Homes are decorated with lamps, and fireworks light up the sky.
